Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

Dr. Abdul Kalam

Below is the timeline of Dr. Kalam's life:

  • 1931 : Born in Rameshvaram, Tamilnadu
  • 1960 : Graduated in Aerospace Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology
  • 1969 : Joined ISRO after working at DRDO.
  • 1980 : Project Director of India's first stellite launch vehicle, SLV-III
  • 1982-1989 : Rejoined DRDO and was responsible for India's frst intermediate ballistic missile, AGNI
  • 1992-1999 : Worked as Chief Adviser to Defence Minister
  • 2002-2007 : President of India
  • 2012 : Died at the age of 83 years

Awards and Honours Received by Dr. Kalam:

  • Kalam received 7 honorary doctorates from 40 universities.
  • Honoured with Padma Bhushan in 1981, for his work in ISRO
  • Honoured with Padma Vibhushan in 1990, for his work in DRDO and his role as Chief Adviser
  • Received India's highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna, for his contribution to the scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India.
  • In 2013, he was the recipient of the Von Braun Award from the National Space Society "to recognize excellence in the management and leadership of a space-related project".
  • Ranked number 2 in Outlook India's poll of the Greatest Indian.
